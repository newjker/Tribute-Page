Alan Mathison Turing

The man who invented the first computer

Here's a time line of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912 Born in Maida Vale, London, England
  • 1918-21 Enrolled a Primary School at 20 Charles Road, St Leonards-on-Sea
  • 1922-26 Attended Hazelhurst Preparatoty Scool in Sussex
  • 1926 Attended Sherborne Scool in Dorset
  • 1927 Move to Guildford with his parents
  • 1928 Discovered about Albert Einstein and his work
  • 1930 Alan's bestfriend, Christopher Morcom died from bovine tuberculosis
  • 1931-34 Attended at King's College, Cambridge
  • 1935 Alan was elected a Fellow of King's College
  • 1936 Turing published his paper "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem"
  • 1936-38 Alan spent his time studying at Princeton University
  • 1938 Turing obtained his PhD from the Department of Mathematics at Princeton
  • 1938 Started working for GC&CS
